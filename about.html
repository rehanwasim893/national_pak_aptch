About Karachi National Park

Karachi National Park is located in the heart of Karachi, Sindh. The park is an oasis of nature amidst the bustling city life, providing a habitat for a variety of wildlife. Covering an area of approximately 15 sq km, it is a perfect getaway for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts.

The park is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including several species of birds, mammals, and reptiles. It offers a peaceful environment for visitors to enjoy a walk, picnic, or wildlife watching. Karachi National Park is known for its scenic beauty, wildlife conservation efforts, and its role in preserving the natural heritage of Karachi.

History of Karachi National Park

Karachi National Park was established in 1985 by the Government of Sindh to conserve the rich biodiversity of the region. The park was created to protect the natural habitat of various species and to promote eco-tourism. It is one of the most important green spaces in Karachi, providing both environmental and recreational benefits.

The park is home to many species of birds, mammals, and reptiles. Some of the most popular animals found in the park include wild boar, leopards, foxes, and various species of deer. The park also has a rich population of reptiles, including snakes and lizards, along with a variety of bird species, making it an ideal location for wildlife photography and bird watching.
